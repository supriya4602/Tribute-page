The Government of Maharashtra lists 19 February as a holiday commemorating Shivaji's birth (Shivaji Jayanti).Shivaji was named after a local deity, the goddess Shivai. Shivaji's father Shahaji Bhonsle was a Maratha general who served the Deccan Sultanates. Shivaji Maharaj's mode of warfare


maintained a small but effective standing army. The core of Shivaji's army consisted of peasants of the Maratha and Kunbi castes.Shivaji was aware of the limitations of his army. He realised that conventional warfare methods were inadequate to confront the big, well-trained cavalry of the Mughals which was equipped with field artillery. As a result, Shivaji adopted guerilla tactics which became known as 'Ganimi Kawa'.Shivaji was a master of guerrilla warfare.[156] His strategies consistently perplexed and defeated armies sent against him. He realized that the most vulnerable point of the large, slow-moving armies of the time was supply. He utilised knowledge of the local terrain and the superior mobility of his light cavalry to cut off supplies to the enemy.[157] Shivaji refused to confront in pitched battles. Instead, he lured the enemies in difficult hills and jungles of his own choosing, catching them at a disadvantage and routing them. Leadership Lessons from the Legend of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj-

1. We over Me

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, always believed on one philosophy, of We over Me. He not only believed that but, also always practiced it during many critical incidents. He displayed immense ease in exhibiting team spirit, which helped him lead the team in critical difficult situations of life and death


2. Practicing over Preaching

He always believed in practicing over mere preaching. Rather then instructing anyone about some principle, he always lived it through his daily life. There were many instance where he needed the team to lead their lives to be sacrificed for the Matrubhumi/ Mother Land, in such cases he was the first one to do so.


3. Leading from the front Over Instructing from the back

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj always led every task to governing or warfare, from the front i.e. being the front leader to participate, rather then being just an instructor and staying back. This he proved in many occasions such as winning many forts of his time, which were considered impossible.


4. Innovation over Tradition

His war strategies and governing strategies always carried the touch of innovativeness. Looking at the limited resource always faced during his lifetime, he always believed in effective utilisation of people and any natural resource including warfare material and weapons. His warfare style and governing style has been always the topic of research for all military related and governance related topics of research, needless to say also for the competitive strategies in business as well.


5. Entrustment over Encroachment

Once he entrusted any activity to anyone, he always trusted him or her fully. he never encroached in his work, rather awaited for the person to respond back. He always alllowed anyone to commit mistakes, so that the fear of failure, goes off and fearlessness could be established along side the warrior attitude. The reason of this was that he could percolate the vision in hearts of all mates.
